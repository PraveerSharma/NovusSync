## 1. How to use this register

Every recommendation below remains a proposal unless its row and the accepted-decision record explicitly mark it accepted. The source brief is pre-development and calls its other choices “working assumptions” or “recommended defaults.” An implementation agent must not interpret an open recommendation as authorization.

To close an item:

1. Record the selected option, founder/decision owner, decision date, evidence, and consequences.
2. Change its status from `Open` to `Accepted`, `Rejected`, or `Deferred`.
3. Create an ADR for a material or hard-to-reverse technical choice.
4. Update `MVP_SCOPE.md`, architecture, roadmap, and backlog if the accepted choice changes them.

## 2. Decision summary and critical path

The following sequence minimizes rework:

1. Resolve customer and outcome: OD-001 through OD-006.
2. Resolve product boundary and operating model: OD-007 through OD-014.
3. Complete the accepted foundation architecture/toolchain/privacy spikes for OD-015 through OD-017, OD-023 through OD-030, and OD-035 through OD-037.
4. Execute the accepted OD-020 partner booking inventory/provider spike, OD-021 verified Business Brain boundary, and OD-022 deterministic creative boundary; complete OD-033 readiness. OD-018/OD-019 Meta APIs are explicitly deferred under ADR-0019.
5. Approve commercial constraints and delivery capacity: OD-014, OD-031, and OD-032.
6. Convert accepted technical recommendations into ADRs before implementation.

As of this document, **all 37 decisions are classified: 34 accepted and three deferred, with no open decision**. OD-018/OD-019/OD-034 are deferred with explicit MVP defaults. Decision closure is not an execution pass: **application scaffolding remains blocked by Gate G0 evidence**, and **real-data/live-pilot work remains blocked** until the accepted OD-033 [live-readiness checklist](../readiness/OD-033_LIVE_READINESS.md) plus OD-027/OD-032 implementation evidence passes.
