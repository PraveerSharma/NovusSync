# Eligible-Owner Interview Script

**Length:** 25–35 minutes  
**Goal:** Learn what happened in the owner's real workflow before describing the proposed solution.  
**Evidence record:** Use the coded interview record in [Gate G0 §4](GATE_G0_EXECUTION.md#4-eligible-owner-interview-record).

## Before the call

- Confirm that the participant owns or has final operating/marketing authority for an independent Bengaluru yoga studio.
- Assign a participant/studio code in the private workspace.
- Do not request customer names, phone numbers, raw chats, passwords, account access, or health information.
- Do not record audio/video unless the participant gives explicit permission and the approved private storage/retention route exists. Written notes are the default.
- Prepare to listen. The product pitch comes only after the research section is finished.

## 1. Opening, 2 minutes

> Thank you for speaking with me. I am researching the current process before building the product. I want to understand what actually happened in your most recent campaign, including anything that works well. I do not need customer names or private messages. May I take written notes and use an anonymous summary for product decisions?

If permission is not given, do not retain notes beyond what is needed to close the conversation.

## 2. Eligibility and operating context, 3–5 minutes

1. What type of yoga studio do you run, and how many locations or instructors are involved?
2. What introductory offer or first attended experience do new prospects usually book?
3. Who makes the final decision on marketing, pricing, account access, and customer follow-up?
4. Which Instagram account, WhatsApp setup, and booking method does the business currently use?

Stop or mark ineligible if the business falls outside the accepted MVP boundary. Do not bend the boundary to save an interview.

## 3. Most recent campaign, 8–10 minutes

1. Think about the most recent Instagram campaign or promotion that produced an enquiry. What was the offer and when did it run?
2. Please walk me through what happened from publishing the post to the first enquiry.
3. Where did the enquiry arrive: a form, WhatsApp, Instagram, phone, or somewhere else?
4. Who noticed it, how quickly, and what did they do next?
5. What information had to be copied, remembered, or asked again?
6. Where did follow-up stop or become uncertain?
7. Can you recall one recent enquiry that was late, missed, duplicated, or hard to follow? What happened and what did it cost in time or opportunity?
8. What part of this process already works well enough that you would not want it changed?

Do not suggest the two target pains. Let the owner reveal them through the story.

## 4. Booking, attendance, and attribution, 6–8 minutes

1. How does a qualified person see available trial classes and make a booking?
2. Which system or person owns schedules, locations, instructors, class capacity, rescheduling, and cancellations?
3. How do you know whether the person actually attended?
4. Can you connect that attendance back to the exact campaign or offer? Show or describe the last time you tried.
5. What happens when the source or attendance is unknown or disputed?
6. Which numbers do you review after a campaign, and which numbers do you wish you trusted more?

## 5. Baseline and workload, 4–5 minutes

1. For the most recent four weeks, which of these can you retrieve: enquiries, first-response time, bookings, attendance, enrollment/loss, spend, and owner/staff hours?
2. Where is each number stored, and who can verify it?
3. Roughly how much owner/staff time per week goes into posts, replies, follow-up, booking reconciliation, and reporting?
4. What would prevent you from sharing a minimized baseline and de-identified outcome summary for a pilot?

“I think we have it somewhere” is a gap, not usable baseline evidence.

## 6. Priority and willingness to change, 4–6 minutes

Ask these before revealing detailed pricing:

1. Compared with the other problems in the business, how urgent is this one? What is ahead of it?
2. What have you already tried or paid for? Why did you keep or stop it?
3. If nothing changes for the next three months, what happens?
4. Who else must agree before you can test a new managed workflow?

Then give the 60-second concept:

> We are testing a managed 28-day campaign workflow. The studio approves all facts and content, humans publish Instagram posts and handle WhatsApp in the official apps, the studio's booking route remains authoritative, and we track the path to a verified attended trial. There is no Meta automation and no guaranteed outcome. The first-five price is INR 4,999 through 100 new Leads or INR 6,999 for 101–200, with an INR 1,000 deposit credited to the first cycle after the documents and payment route are approved.

Ask:

5. What feels useful, unclear, risky, or unnecessary?
6. At that price and operating model, what would have to be true for you to run two cycles?
7. Would you review the design-partner brief and readiness requirements? Why or why not?

Do not count “sounds interesting” as willingness to pay.

## 7. Close, 2 minutes

- Ask permission to send a short anonymous summary back for correction.
- Ask for one relevant owner referral, without pressure.
- If eligible and serious, offer the [design-partner brief](DESIGN_PARTNER_BRIEF.md).
- State the exact next step and owner. Do not promise a launch date.

## 8. Evidence scoring after the call

| Signal | `YES` requires | `NO` or `UNKNOWN` when |
|---|---|---|
| Pain 1: slow/fragmented follow-up | A recent real incident or inspectable workflow showing delay, missed context, repeated entry, inconsistent handoff, or follow-up loss | Only a general opinion, hypothetical problem, or interviewer-led agreement |
| Pain 2: campaign-to-attendance gap | A recent real inability or manual burden connecting campaign/offer to booking and verified attendance | Reach/leads are considered sufficient, or the link is already reliable with low effort |
| Usable four-week baseline | Named sources and an authorized person can provide the agreed fields for a defined four-week period | Data is remembered, incomplete, unverifiable, or inaccessible |
| Willingness to commit | Owner accepts a concrete next step toward signed two-cycle participation | Compliments, demo requests, or “maybe later” without a next step |
| Paid evidence | Approved first-cycle payment or credited INR 1,000 deposit is completed after required reviews | Price acceptance, verbal intent, or an unapproved transfer |

Also record counter-evidence, current workaround strength, access burden, regulated/privacy concern, and whether the interview introduced a new material finding.

## 9. Batch review after interview 10

- Count at most one included eligible interview per `studio_code`; one code represents one owner-operated business across all of its locations.
- Keep genuine repeat/follow-up rows only with an explicit denominator-exclusion reason. Never exclude an eligible `NO` merely because it weakens the result.
- Both-pain rate = included unique eligible studio-owner records with `YES` for both pains / all included unique eligible studio-owner records.
- Report numerator and denominator, not a rounded claim alone.
- List the three strongest counterexamples and three strongest repeated patterns.
- Continue toward 20 if new material workflows, objections, or contradictions are still appearing.
- Do not invite the founder to sign `GO` until the partner, manual-workflow, prototype, feasibility, and contradiction rows also pass.
