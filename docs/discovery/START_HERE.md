# Gate G0: Start Here

**Current state:** Preparation is complete. The founder's one-week field research is pending, and customer evidence has not yet been recorded.  
**Plain-language goal:** Prove that Bengaluru yoga-studio owners repeatedly have the problem, will share enough evidence to solve it, and will make a real commitment before application development starts.

## What happens next

1. Create the founder-approved restricted Google Drive folder and Google Sheet for names, contact details, interview notes, signed documents, and payment references. None of that belongs in Git.
2. Build a list of 25–30 eligible Bengaluru yoga studios. Start with warm introductions, then use respectful public outreach to fill the gap.
3. Complete at least 10 interviews from unique eligible owner-operated studios using the same questions. Continue toward 20 if new findings are still appearing.
4. Check the result honestly. At least 70% must provide real examples of both slow/fragmented lead follow-up and weak campaign-to-attendance tracking.
5. Invite the strongest candidates to become design partners. Five must sign for two intended cycles, three must prove baseline/channel/outcome readiness, and two must pay or place the credited INR 1,000 deposit through an approved route.
6. Observe the whole campaign-to-attendance process manually at two studios and test the clickable prototype with five owners.
7. Review contradictions and record `GO`, `NO-GO`, or `REWORK` in the [Gate G0 record](GATE_G0_EXECUTION.md#8-founder-gate-g0-decision-record).

## Founder operating choices recorded

### Private discovery workspace — accepted 14 July 2026

Use one restricted Google Drive folder plus one Google Sheet, accessible only to the founder and the two named operators, with narrower counsel/accountant access when required. The repository stores only anonymous studio codes and aggregate results.

### Prospect access — accepted 14 July 2026

Start with the founder's known yoga instructors and ask for owner introductions. The founder leads the approach and research over the coming week. Public research may later fill the list to 25–30 candidates if warm introductions are insufficient.

## Decision still needed before signatures or money

Approve an accountant and qualified India counsel to review the participation document, invoice/deposit route, privacy notice, operator terms, refund language, and applicable OD-033 items. The recruitment brief is not a contract and must not be used to collect money by itself.

## Recommendations

- Interview before pitching. A sales pitch changes the answer and hides the real workflow.
- Ask about the most recent campaign and enquiry, not what the owner “would probably do.”
- Aim for 25–30 candidates to produce 10 unique included eligible studio-owner records and five serious partners. Starting with exactly 10 leaves no room for refusals, repeat follow-ups, or ineligible studios.
- The founder should lead the first five interviews. This is the fastest way to hear the language, objections, and trust concerns directly.
- Do not lower the gate because someone is enthusiastic. A real example, signed commitment, usable baseline, or deposit is evidence; praise is not.

## What remains blocked

- No application scaffold until Gate G0 is signed `GO`.
- No real Lead data, paid/live activation, production providers, AI/telemetry, or global checkout until the applicable OD-033 evidence passes.
- No prospect contact, service purchase, signature request, or payment collection without the founder's explicit authorization and the required review.
