# Prospecting and Outreach Kit

**Purpose:** Secure interviews from at least 10 unique eligible owner-operated studios and identify five credible design partners.  
**Audience:** Independent, owner-operated yoga studios in Bengaluru Urban.  
**Rule:** Store business identities and contact details only in the approved private discovery workspace.

## 1. Who belongs on the list

Include a studio when public information or a warm source suggests that it:

- is an independent yoga-studio business in Bengaluru Urban;
- has an owner or equivalent final decision-maker available;
- offers an introductory class, trial, consultation, or similar attended outcome;
- uses a professional Instagram account and can operate an official WhatsApp Business number;
- has a real booking route or a consistent manual booking process; and
- is likely to have enough recent enquiries to discuss lead response and attendance tracking.

Exclude or flag separately:

- franchises, marketplaces, broad gyms/wellness businesses, or agencies managing multiple brands;
- medical, therapeutic, rehabilitation, child-directed, or regulated-claim-led services;
- studios outside Bengaluru Urban;
- businesses without an attended introductory outcome; and
- contacts who cannot make or sponsor the decision.

## 2. Recommended sourcing order

1. Founder, operator, friend, customer, instructor, and local-business introductions.
2. Studios already known through classes, communities, events, or professional networks.
3. Public studio websites, Google Business profiles, and professional Instagram pages.
4. Referrals requested at the end of completed interviews.

Build 25–30 candidates. Target at least 15 scheduled conversations so refusals, no-shows, and eligibility failures still leave 10 completed eligible interviews.

## 4. Outreach rules

- The founder sends first contact to warm relationships. A named operator may schedule after the owner responds.
- Say that this is research before a product build. Do not imply the product is live or proven.
- Ask for 25–30 minutes with the owner or final decision-maker.
- Do not promise leads, attendance, enrollment, revenue, platform compliance, automation, or exclusivity.
- Do not collect customer/Lead data during outreach.
- Stop immediately after a decline or opt-out. If there is no response, send at most two follow-ups, roughly three and seven days after the first message.
- Use the owner's preferred language only when a fluent human can conduct and record the interview accurately. The formal pilot remains English-first.

## 5. Message templates

### Warm introduction request

> Hi [referrer name], I am researching how independent yoga studios in Bengaluru handle Instagram enquiries, WhatsApp follow-up, trial bookings, and attendance tracking. I want to speak with owners before building anything. Would you be comfortable introducing me to [owner/studio] for a 25–30 minute research conversation? I will not ask you to share their customer data.

### Direct first message

> Hi [owner name], I am studying how independent yoga studios in Bengaluru turn Instagram and WhatsApp enquiries into attended trial classes. I am speaking with owners before building the product, so this is a research conversation rather than a sales demo. Could I ask you about the most recent campaign and how your team followed it through to booking and attendance? It would take about 25–30 minutes, and I do not need any customer names or messages.

### Follow-up one

> Hi [owner name], following up on my request for a short owner-research conversation about enquiry follow-up and trial-attendance tracking. I am looking for the real workflow, including what already works well. If this is not relevant, just say so and I will close the loop.

### Final follow-up

> Hi [owner name], this is my final follow-up. I am completing the first set of Bengaluru yoga-studio interviews and would still value your experience. If the timing is not right, no action is needed and I will not follow up again.

### Referral request after an interview

> Thank you. Is there one other independent Bengaluru yoga-studio owner who sees enough Instagram or WhatsApp enquiries to have a useful view on this workflow? Please introduce us only if you are comfortable and they are open to it.

## 6. Invitation after a strong interview

Only use this after the research questions are complete and the owner meets the eligibility boundary.

> Your workflow looks close to the problem we are testing. We are selecting five design partners for a small, hands-on pilot. The next step is not a commitment: I can share a one-page brief explaining the two 28-day cycles, what the studio controls, what we operate manually, the INR 4,999/6,999 tiers, and the evidence we would need. Would you like to review it?

Use the [design-partner brief](DESIGN_PARTNER_BRIEF.md). A positive reply is not a signed commitment and does not count toward Gate G0.
